## Profile 1: GM Elena Volkov

### Basic Information
- **Name**: GM Elena Volkov
- **Title**: Grandmaster (GM)
- **FIDE Rating**: 2587
- **Age**: 34
- **Location**: Barcelona, Spain (UTC+1)
- **Languages**: English (fluent), Spanish (fluent), Russian (native)
- **Hourly Rate**: $120/hour

### Background & Credentials
Elena Volkov earned her Grandmaster title at age 22 and has competed in three Women's World Championship cycles, reaching the quarterfinals in 2019. She holds a degree in Psychology from Moscow State University and has been coaching full-time since 2018. Elena has trained five students to International Master (IM) level and specializes in strategic understanding and psychological preparation for tournament play.

### Teaching Philosophy
**"Chess is 80% psychology, 20% calculation."**

Elena believes that technical knowledge means nothing without the mental framework to apply it under pressure. Her coaching focuses on building pattern recognition, developing a personal opening repertoire, and cultivating the psychological resilience needed for competitive success. She uses a Socratic method, asking students to explain their thought process before offering corrections.

### Specializations
- **Tournament Preparation**: Pre-game analysis, opponent research, opening preparation
- **Strategic Mastery**: Pawn structures, piece coordination, long-term planning
- **Psychological Training**: Tilt management, time pressure handling, confidence building
- **Opening Systems**: Sicilian Defense (Black), Queen's Gambit (White), Caro-Kann

### Teaching Style
- **Archetype**: The Strategist (pattern-based, conceptual understanding)
- **Pace**: Moderate (allows time for reflection and discussion)
- **Feedback Style**: Socratic questioning, encourages self-analysis
- **Lesson Structure**: 10 min review → 40 min deep dive on one concept → 10 min homework assignment

### Ideal Student
- **Rating Range**: 1800-2400 FIDE
- **Goals**: Tournament performance, IM/GM norms, strategic improvement
- **Commitment Level**: Serious competitors willing to study 10+ hours/week
- **Age**: Adults and advanced juniors (16+)

### Availability
- **Days**: Monday-Friday
- **Times**: 14:00-22:00 UTC+1 (flexible for tournament students)
- **Booking**: 48-hour advance notice required
- **Packages**: 4-lesson package (5% discount), 8-lesson package (10% discount)

### Student Testimonials (Fictional)

- **Location**: Buenos Aires, Argentina (UTC-3)

Maria is especially skilled at working with students who feel intimidated by chess or have had negative experiences with overly critical coaches.

## Design Notes

### Visual Representation Strategy
- **Elena**: Professional tournament photo, serious expression, European aesthetic
- **David**: Casual tech-professional vibe, friendly smile, modern background
- **Maria**: Warm and approachable, teaching environment, inclusive energy

### Diversity Considerations
- **Gender**: 2 women, 1 man (reflects chess community diversity goals)
- **Geography**: Europe, North America, South America (global platform)
- **Age Range**: 28-42 (shows coaching is for all career stages)
- **Price Range**: $45-120 (accessible to different budgets)
- **Teaching Styles**: Strategist, Technician, Mentor (different learning preferences)

### Credibility Factors
- **Realistic ratings**: Not all 2700 GMs (more relatable)
- **Specific achievements**: Concrete accomplishments, not vague claims
- **Authentic testimonials**: Realistic praise with specific details
- **Personality quirks**: Fun facts make them feel real, not generic

### Launch Strategy
These three profiles demonstrate:
1. **Range**: Platform serves beginners through advanced players
2. **Value**: Quality coaching at multiple price points
3. **Expertise**: Different specializations for different needs
4. **Accessibility**: Coaches available across time zones and schedules
