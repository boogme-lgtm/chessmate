## Slide 2: The Creator Platform Opportunity
**Chess coaching can learn from creator platforms that enable 2-3x higher earnings**

The most successful creator platforms combine three revenue streams rather than relying on single transaction commissions. OnlyFans creators with $8 subscriptions earn $4,000-8,000 monthly through content upsells—proving that low-friction entry plus strategic upsells maximizes lifetime value.

**Key Market Insights:**
- Direct-to-consumer platforms (OnlyFans, Patreon, Substack) charge 10-20% and let creators keep 80-90%
- Discovery marketplaces charge 15-70% depending on who brings the customer
- Winning model: Dynamic pricing based on value delivered (Leland charges 20% for platform leads, 5% for coach referrals)
- Traditional chess academies take 70-80%, leaving coaches with only 20-30%
- Modern platforms enable coaches to earn 3-4x more than academy employment

**BooGMe positioning:** Full-service marketplace with creator economics—not just a listing board, but complete transaction management with minimal platform fees.

## Slide 3: Revised Fee Structure (Skool-Inspired Model)
**Coaches choose their membership tier based on volume and features needed**

**Free Tier: "Starter Coach"**
- Monthly fee: $0
- Transaction fee: 15% on all bookings
- Features: Basic profile, calendar integration, payment processing, standard analytics
- Best for: New coaches testing the platform, part-time coaches (<10 hours/week)

**Professional Tier: "Growth Coach"**
- Monthly fee: $49/month
- Transaction fee: 8% on all bookings
- Features: Everything in Starter + content library hosting (50GB), group lesson tools (up to 10 students), priority search placement, advanced analytics, custom branding
- Best for: Active coaches (10-20 hours/week), coaches building a brand
- Break-even: 7 hours of lessons per month at $80/hour

**Elite Tier: "Business Coach"**
- Monthly fee: $149/month
- Transaction fee: 2-5% on all bookings
- Features: Everything in Professional + unlimited content hosting, unlimited group size, top search placement, API access, white-label options, dedicated account manager
- Best for: Full-time coaches (20+ hours/week), coaches with existing audience, GMs
- Break-even: 15 hours of lessons per month at $80/hour

**Financial Example (20 hours/week coach at $80/hour):**
- Free tier: $6,400 monthly revenue - 15% = $5,440 take-home
- Professional tier: $6,400 - $49 - 8% = $5,839 take-home (+$399/month, +7%)
- Elite tier: $6,400 - $149 - 5% = $6,931 take-home (+$1,491/month, +27%)

**Messaging to coaches:** "Choose the plan that fits your volume—minimal platform fees that decrease as you grow"

## Slide 4: Three Revenue Streams for Coaches
**Maximize earnings by combining live lessons, subscriptions, and content monetization**

**Stream 1: Live 1-on-1 Lessons (Core Offering)**
- Coach sets rate: $50-200/hour typical
- Student books individual lessons with escrow protection
- Platform provides: Payment processing, scheduling, video infrastructure, recording, refund protection
- Platform fee: 2-15% based on coach membership tier
- Best for: Personalized instruction, specific problem-solving, high-touch coaching

**Stream 2: Student Subscriptions (Recurring Revenue)**
- Coach sets monthly price: $150-400/month suggested
- Includes: 4-6 lessons per month (pre-booked at discount), access to coach's content library, priority scheduling, group lesson access, community forum
- Platform provides: Subscription management, content hosting, community tools, streaming infrastructure
- Platform fee: Same as live lessons (2-15% based on tier)
- Best for: Serious students, tournament players, consistent learners seeking regular coaching

**Stream 3: Content Monetization (Scalable Income)**
- Base subscription: $30-50/month (group lessons, community access, basic content)
- Pay-per-view unlocks: Opening courses ($25-75), game analysis packs ($40), tournament prep courses ($100-150)
- Custom requests: Opening prep for specific opponent ($100-200), personalized training plans ($150/month)
- Platform provides: Content hosting, paywall system, streaming, download protection
- Platform fee: Same as live lessons (2-15% based on tier)
- Best for: Scaling beyond time constraints, passive income, reaching larger audience

- Price: $200-300/month (coach-set, platform suggests based on lesson rate)
- Access: 4-6 lessons per month (discounted rate), access to coach's content library, priority scheduling, group lesson access, community forum, reduced rate for additional lessons
- Best for: Serious improvers, club players, consistent training schedule
- Value proposition: 20-30% savings vs pay-per-lesson, plus bonus content and community

**Premium: "Tournament Player"**
- Price: $800-1,500/month
- Access: 8-12 lessons per month, everything in Committed tier, opening preparation database, game analysis within 24 hours, tournament seconding (remote), direct messaging access
- Best for: Competitive players, pre-tournament intensive prep, titled players
- Value proposition: Comprehensive support system, coach as team member

## Slide 11: Risk Mitigation Strategies
**Anticipate common marketplace challenges and build solutions into the model**

**Risk 1: Coaches bypass platform after first lesson**
- Likelihood: High (common marketplace problem)
- Mitigation: Low transaction fees (2-8% for paid tiers) make it attractive to stay on-platform, escrow protection benefits both parties, subscription model creates ongoing value, non-compete clause for platform-sourced students
- Messaging: "Keep your students on-platform—we handle payments, scheduling, and protection for minimal fees"

**Risk 2: Students unwilling to pay subscription + PPV**
- Likelihood: Medium
- Mitigation: Make subscription optional (not required), test pricing with beta users, provide clear value differentiation, start with lower prices and increase based on demand
- Messaging: "Pay as you go, or subscribe for savings—your choice"

**Risk 3: Content creation burden too high for coaches**
- Likelihood: Medium-High
- Mitigation: Provide templates and examples, allow repurposing of lesson recordings, make content optional (coaches can be lessons-only), offer content creation service (platform creates content from lessons for revenue share)
- Messaging: "Content is optional—focus on teaching, we'll help with the rest"

**Risk 4: Complexity confuses users**
- Likelihood: Medium
- Mitigation: Phased rollout (don't launch everything at once), clear user journeys for different personas, default to simplest path (single lesson booking), progressive disclosure of advanced features
- Messaging: "Start simple, grow when ready"

**Risk 5: Price sensitivity in chess market**
- Likelihood: Medium
- Mitigation: Emphasize "minimal platform fees" rather than percentages, show coach earnings comparison vs academies, highlight student savings vs traditional coaching, offer Free tier with no monthly cost
- Messaging: "Fair fees that benefit both coaches and students"

**Visual:** Risk matrix (Likelihood vs Impact) with mitigation strategies as arrows moving risks to lower-left quadrant

## Slide 13: Key Takeaways & Next Steps
**Strategic decisions needed to move forward**

**Core Strategy:**
1. **Lower fees, higher volume**: 2-15% transaction fees (vs industry 15-70%) offset by membership revenue and network effects
2. **Skool-inspired membership model**: Coaches choose monthly fee + low transaction rate vs no fee + higher rate
3. **Three revenue streams**: Live lessons (core) + subscriptions (recurring) + content (scalable) = 2-3x coach earnings
4. **Creator economics**: Coaches keep 85-98%, comparable to Patreon/OnlyFans, far better than academies (20-30%)
5. **Messaging**: "Minimal platform fees" rather than highlighting percentages—let value speak first

**Immediate Actions (Next 30 Days):**
1. Finalize coach membership tier pricing and features
2. Survey current waitlist: Would coaches pay $49-149/month for lower fees? Would students pay $200-300/month for subscriptions?
3. Prototype membership selection flow in the platform
4. Update coaches landing page with new membership tier messaging
5. Create coach comparison calculator: "See how much you'd earn on BooGMe vs academies/other platforms"

**Strategic Decisions Needed:**
1. **Launch timing**: All three tiers at once, or start with Free tier and add paid tiers after validation?
2. **Subscription timing**: Launch subscriptions in Phase 1 or wait until Phase 3?
3. **Content priority**: Core feature from day 1 or add after marketplace is proven?

**Recommended Approach:**
- Start with all three coach membership tiers (Free, Professional, Elite) to serve different coach segments
- Perfect lesson marketplace first (Phases 1-2), add subscriptions and content in Phases 3-4
- Build architecture to support future features from day 1, but don't expose complexity until validated

**The Vision:**
BooGMe becomes the platform where chess coaches earn 3-4x more than traditional employment while students access world-class instruction at fair prices—powered by creator economics, not extractive marketplace fees.
