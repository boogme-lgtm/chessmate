# Creator Platform Economics Research

## Commission Rates by Platform Type

### Content Creator Platforms
- **OnlyFans**: 20% platform fee (creators keep 80%)
- **Patreon**: 5-12% platform fee + payment processing (creators keep 88-95%)
- **Substack**: 10% platform fee (creators keep 90%)
- **YouTube**: 45% of ad revenue (creators keep 55%)
- **TikTok**: Variable, view-based

### Education/Course Platforms
- **Udemy**: 
  - Organic sales: Instructors keep 37% (Udemy takes 63%)
  - Instructor-driven sales: Instructors keep 97% (Udemy takes 3%)
  - Udemy Connect 1-1: Instructors keep 80% (Udemy takes 20%)
- **Teachable**: $39-$119/month subscription (no commission) OR commission-based
- **Skillshare**: Royalty pool model based on watch time

### Tutoring/Coaching Marketplaces
- **General tutoring platforms**: 15-25% commission typical
- **High-end platforms**: Can take up to 70% for premium services
- **Leland (coaching marketplace)**: 
  - Standard: 20% commission
  - Coach-referred clients: 5% commission
  - Ongoing revenue share on referred clients

## Key Insights

1. **Direct-to-consumer platforms** (OnlyFans, Substack, Patreon) charge 10-20% and let creators keep 80-90%

2. **Marketplace discovery platforms** (Udemy, tutoring sites) charge 15-70% depending on who brings the customer

3. **Hybrid models** (Leland) reward coaches for bringing their own clients with lower rates (5% vs 20%)

4. **Payment processing** adds 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction on top of platform fees

## Payout Timing
- **OnlyFans**: 7-21 day hold, then available for withdrawal
- **Patreon**: Monthly on 1st, 1-5 day processing
- **Substack**: Rolling (Stripe-based), no minimum
- **YouTube**: Monthly (21st-26th), $100 minimum

## Leland Case Study: $10M+ Coaching Marketplace

### Business Model
- **Started**: MBA admissions coaching (niche-first approach)
- **Revenue**: $10M+ annually
- **Coaches**: 2,000+
- **Reviews**: 16,000+ public reviews

### Commission Structure
- **Standard rate**: Coaches keep 80-90% (platform takes 10-20%)
- **Traditional firms**: Coaches only kept 20-30% (firms took 70-80%)
- **Coach-referred clients**: Lower commission (5% mentioned in search results)

### Key Success Factors
1. **High supply-side standards**: Reject 90% of coach applicants
2. **Price range diversity**: $30/hour to $400+/hour coaches
3. **Multi-modal offering**: 1-on-1 coaching, cohort courses, content
4. **Strategic expansion**: Follow customer journey (MBA → consulting prep → career coaching)
5. **Organic growth**: 30%+ from word of mouth

### Value Proposition
- **For clients**: Half the price of traditional firms, transparent reviews
- **For coaches**: 3-4x higher take-home vs traditional agencies, client access

### Expansion Strategy
- Start with "desperate-to-have" problems (high-stakes, complex, clear success metrics)
- Let user behavior guide new categories
- Map customer journeys to find adjacent needs
- Only expand after building trust in core category
