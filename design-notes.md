# BooGMe Design Research & Color Palette

## C-Squared Branding Analysis

From the C-Squared shop and YouTube channel:
- **Primary Color**: Warm terracotta/burnt orange overlay (#C27A4A approximately)
- **Logo**: Minimalist geometric "C²" design with white and maroon/red squares
- **Aesthetic**: Warm, professional, chess-focused
- **Photography**: Sepia/warm toned overlays on imagery

## Proposed BooGMe Color Palette

### Swiss Modern + Neo-Minimal with Earthy Tones

**Primary Colors:**
- **Burgundy/Wine** (#722F37) - Deep, sophisticated maroon - primary accent
- **Terracotta** (#C27A4A) - Warm earthy tone - secondary accent
- **Charcoal** (#1A1A1A) - Near-black for text and dark mode backgrounds

**Neutral Colors:**
- **Warm White** (#FAF8F5) - Off-white with warm undertone for backgrounds
- **Stone** (#E8E4DF) - Light warm gray for cards and sections
- **Slate** (#6B6B6B) - Medium gray for secondary text

**Accent Colors:**
- **Gold** (#B8860B) - For achievements, premium features
- **Forest** (#2D5A4A) - Subtle green for success states

## Design Principles

### Swiss Modern Elements:
- Strong grid-based layout (12-column)
- Bold, clear typography hierarchy
- Generous whitespace
- Functional, purposeful design
- High contrast for readability

### Neo-Minimal Elements:
- Clean lines and geometric shapes
- Limited color palette (max 3-4 colors)
- Subtle shadows and depth
- Micro-interactions on hover
- Dark mode option with same palette

### Typography:
- Headlines: Inter or Satoshi (geometric sans-serif)
- Body: Inter (clean, highly legible)
- Monospace: JetBrains Mono (for ratings, stats)

## Key Design Changes from Current:
1. Remove cyberpunk glow effects
2. Replace cyan/magenta with burgundy/terracotta
3. Add more whitespace
4. Use grid-based card layouts
5. Simplify animations
6. Add warm tones to imagery
